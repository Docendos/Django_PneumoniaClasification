Diagnostic methods

Diagnostic methods refer to the various procedures and tests that doctors use to determine the underlying cause of a patient's symptoms. These tests help doctors to arrive at an accurate diagnosis, which is essential for developing an effective treatment plan. The choice of diagnostic method varies depending on the patient's symptoms and medical history.

One of the most common diagnostic methods is the physical examination. During this procedure, a doctor will use their hands, eyes, and ears to gather information about the patient's symptoms. They may use a stethoscope to listen to the patient's heartbeat and lungs or examine the patient's skin for any abnormalities. A physical examination is a non-invasive method of diagnosis that can provide a wealth of information about the patient's health. Blood tests are another common diagnostic method.
